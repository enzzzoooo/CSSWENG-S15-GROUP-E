Analysis of Axial Deformations in a Structure
Introduction and lesson objectives
In the previous lesson, we have determined the relationship between stress and strain. Under linear elastic behavior, materials follow Hooke’s law, which in its simplest form is written as \(\sigma = E\epsilon\). Based on Hooke’s law, we have derived an equation that relates the deformation and the normal force in an axially loaded bar. When such bars in a particular structure deform axially, this results to a change in the positions of both rigid and deformable members in that structure. In this lesson, we will be analyzing how the axial deformations relate to the deflections of certain points in a structure, applying geometric principles.

At the end of this lesson, you should be able to:
  1. Differentiate deformation and deflection;
  2. Draw deformation diagrams; and
  3. Write geometric equations that relate the axial deformations of members to the deflections of a particular point in a structure.
